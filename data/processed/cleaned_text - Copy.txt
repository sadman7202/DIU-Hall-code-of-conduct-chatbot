HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
1. Hall Admission is open to full-time students of DIU.
Preference will be given to out-station students and newly
admitted students.
2. Students can book a seat either online or by submitting an
application to the respective Hall Office during the office hours
for manual booking.Incomplete or providing false information
in application will be canceled

--- PAGE 6 ---
HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
3.Selected applicants for Hall admission will be notified via letter, email, or a
notice on the university website at least three (03) days before the semester
begins. Once the seat is confirmed, the applicant must occupy the
accommodation within the given deadline. Additionally, the required
payment must be completed within three (03) working days of receiving the
confirmation. Failure to do so will result in cancellation of the allotment and
the application will be considered invalid.

--- PAGE 7 ---
HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
4.Hall admission is valid for one academic semester only. To continue for the
next semester, students must renew their seat through the online portal and
submit payment confirmation by the scheduled deadline. When circulated by
the Hall authority, students must also provide their consent to continue using
the Hall facilities. Additionally, students must maintain a minimum CGPA of
2.5 to retain their seat. If a student fails to meet this requirement, their seat
may be cancelled, or the Provost may take appropriate action as deemed
necessary.

--- PAGE 8 ---
HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
5.Students must apply for seat cancellation for the next semester by the
specified deadline and clear all outstanding dues before doing so. A penalty
of Tk. 1000 will be charged for late cancellations. After cancellation, the
student must vacate the hall and return the room and all allotted items to the
Hall Office in proper condition.
6.Refund policy: Refund of hall fees is allowed only upon cancellation of DIU
admission. Tk. 3000/- will be deducted for processing charges if canceled
before classes start; no refund is available after commencement of class or
after the accommodation period begins.

--- PAGE 9 ---
HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
7.No student will be allowed to continue occupying a hall seat if he/she
discontinues academic activities or fails to register for the ongoing academic
semester. In such cases, the hall seat will be subject to cancellation. If a
student needs to withdraw from an academic semester for valid and
documented reasons, he/she must seek prior approval from the Provost by
submitting a formal application to request permission to continue residing in
the Hall. Furthermore, the student is required to pay the Hall fees by the
specified payment deadline.

--- PAGE 10 ---
HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
8. The hall must be vacated with all personal belongings during semester
breaks or extended leaves such as Eid, Puja, or other religious festivals.
Students are solely responsible for their valuables, including mobile phones,
laptops, personal computers, or cash, and must carry them at their own risk.
9.The Hall fee for the next academic semester is required to be paid during the
seat renewal schedule or before. late fee of Tk. 500 will be charged for
payments made after the due date (for seat renewal or semester payment).

--- PAGE 11 ---
HALL ADMISSION, SEAT ALLOCATION & RENEWAL POLICY
10.Room or Hall change without the prior consent of the Provost is strictly
prohibited. Any student wishing to change their room or hall must submit a
formal application to the Provost and, upon approval, pay a fee of Tk. 3000
as room/hall change charges. However, if the Provost finds the reason for the
change to be justified and reasonable, the fee may be waived at the Provost’s
discretion.
11.Creative International (CI) management reserves the right to revise hall fees
structure when deemed necessary, with prior notice provided to all concerned
parties.

--- PAGE 12 ---
VISITORS AND GUEST ACCOMMODATION REGULATIONS
12.Visitors are not permitted to enter student rooms. They must be received in
the Visitors’ Room or Reception Area between 9:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m.
Guest accommodation may be allowed with prior written approval from the
Provost. 
Note: Female guests are not allowed in the Boys’ Halls, and male guests are not
allowed in the Female halls.

--- PAGE 13 ---
STUDENTS ENTRY TIME & NIGHT-OUT REGULATIONS
13.Every male student must return to their respective Hall by 10:00 PM. Female students must
return by 6:00 PM (November to February) and 7:00 PM (March to October). If any student
needs to stay outside the Hall beyond the specified time due to a valid reason, they must obtain
prior written permission from the Provost through the respective House Tutor. Failure to
secure prior permission will result in disciplinary action, including penalties or possible
expulsion from the Hall
14.Students must seek and obtain prior permission from the Provost for any night outs,
which are allowed only for visits to the local guardian or parents' house. Permission
should be requested at least one day in advance.

Room Inspection & Access Authority

any room at any time, with or without prior notice,
including when the student is not present. This may

HALL AUTHORITY SUPERVISION & DISCIPLINARY JURISDICTION
16.The Hall Authority shall not be held responsible for any mishap occurring
outside the hall premises.
17.During their stay in the Hall, they will be under the Supervision of the
respective Hall Provost.
18.All matters related to conflicts among students or allegations against Hall
staff, must be reported to the Provost who will take appropriate action as
deemed necessary. No student is permitted to lodge a police complaint
without obtaining prior permission from the Provost.

--- PAGE 16 ---
ROOM ALLOCATION & PERSONAL BELONGINGS POLICY
19.The Hall Authority will decide the allocation of rooms / seats, furniture, shoe
racks, and hangers. Students are not allowed to bring or use any personal
furniture in their hall rooms.
20.The Hall Authority is not responsible for the safety of students' personal
belongings. Students are advised to use their own locks and take proper care
of their possessions. They must not leave their room keys unattended or in
insecure places. Any loss of personal items will be the sole responsibility of
the student.

--- PAGE 17 ---
HEALTH, ISSUE AND STAFF CONDUCT GUIDELINES
21.Any case of illness, accident, or emergency must be reported immediately to
the Hall Office or to the designated staff on duty that day.
22.Students diagnosed with any contagious disease will not be permitted to stay
in the Hall. The decision of the Provost in such matters will be final and must
be strictly followed.
23.Any complaint, suggestion, or feedback regarding hall services must be
submitted to the Hall Authority through the proper channels. Sharing such
matters on social media is strictly prohibited.
24.No students shall use the service of a Hall staff for personal work even on
payment. He/she shall also not bring any staff from outside even temporarily.

PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
25.Smoking, the consumption of alcoholic drinks or drugs, use of e-
cigarettes, and the selling or trading of drugs are strictly prohibited both
within the hall and across the campus, as we uphold a 100% tobacco-
free environment. Violation of this rule will result in strict disciplinary
action, temporary suspension from the hall, or permanent expulsion
from the university, depending on the severity of the offense.
26.If any student is found engaging in misbehavior, misconduct, ragging, body
shaming, bullying, harassment, discrimination, or intimidation, he/she will be
immediately expelled from the Hall, and any fees paid will be forfeited.
Violators will also face disciplinary actions such as warnings or temporary
suspension depending on the severity of the offense.

--- PAGE 19 ---
PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
27.Residents should not participate in any anti-national, anti-social or
undesirable activity in or outside the campus.
28.If any individual is found engaged in any sexual activities, his/her hall seat
will be immediately canceled. A report will be submitted to the Proctor's
Office for further action.

--- PAGE 20 ---
PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
29.Unlawful protests, shouting, mob gatherings, or any attempt to incite unrest
within the hall or campus premises are strictly prohibited. Students are
expected to voice their concerns through proper channels, such as the

Provost. Any violation of this policy will lead to disciplinary action, which
may include a written warning, guardian notification, temporary suspension
from the hall, or permanent cancellation of hall allotment, depending on the
severity of the offense.

--- PAGE 21 ---
PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
30.All forms of sports or physical games are strictly prohibited inside hall rooms
and in any areas not specifically designated for such activities.
31.No student is allowed to disturb others by playing musical instruments, using
loudspeakers, watching videos or playing games at high volume, shouting, or
engaging in any disruptive activities that may affect the peace and discipline
of the hall. Any such behavior will be treated as a violation of hall rules and
may lead to disciplinary action.

PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS

ENERGY CONSERVATION & PROPERTY RESPONSIBILITY
36.Any damage to hall property, including furniture and fixtures, caused by a
student’s negligence will be charged to the student or the group of students
residing in the concerned room. Any damage to hall property, including
furniture and fixtures, resulting from a student’s negligence will be the
responsibility of the student or the group of students occupying the affected
room. The cost of repairs or replacement will be charged to them
accordingly.
37.Students are expected to switch off lights, fans, and other electrical
appliances when leaving their rooms and are encouraged to take necessary
precautions to conserve electricity

ENERGY CONSERVATION & PROPERTY RESPONSIBILITY
38.Students are prohibited from driving nails, screws, or similar objects into
walls or doors. They are not allowed to attempt any repairs themselves. If
any repairs are needed, students must report the issue to the Hall Office,
which will arrange for the necessary repairs to be carried out.
39.All facilities, including additional amenities such as TV, refrigerator, oven,

--- PAGE 26 ---
DINING COMPLAINT GUIDELINES
40.Students must lodge any dining-related complaints immediately at the hall
office after consuming the food in the dining hall. Complaints will not be
accepted if the food is taken to the room or consumed elsewhere. If
unresolved, the issue may be escalated to the hall management authority by
maintaining the proper chain of command. Complaints made directly to
higher authorities, bypassing the hall office or hall management, will not be
entertained.

--- PAGE 27 ---
CRISIS MANAGEMENT AND STUDENT COOPERATION
41.Power Outages and Generator Failures: Students must remain calm,
composed, and cooperative during any unexpected power failure, generator
malfunction, or technical fault. Misuse of electrical equipment, tampering
with facilities, or interfering with maintenance operations is strictly
prohibited and will be treated as a violation of hall discipline.
42.Water Pump and Lift Malfunctions: In case of water supply interruptions or lift
malfunctions, students must act patiently and responsibly without creating
panic. Hall students should cooperate with hall authorities during maintenance
(Plumbing, Electrical, Furniture, Internet, Generator, etc.) or repair work.
Spreading misinformation, rumors, or unnecessary complaints during technical
issues is strictly discouraged and may result in disciplinary action.

--- PAGE 28 ---
COMPLIANCE WITH HALL RULES & ADMINISTRATIVE RIGHTS
43.Students residing in the Hall are required to strictly follow all rules and
regulations set by the Hall Authority. The Hall Authority also reserves the
absolute right to cancel the admission or allotment of any student found to be
undeserving or unfit for residence, without the obligation to provide any
explanation or justification.
***Important Note: All students are expected to uphold mutual respect,
patience, and discipline during any form of technical disruption within the
residential facilities.
